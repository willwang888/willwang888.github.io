About

Hi there! As you may know by now, my name is William Wang. Whether you may have stumbled upon my website or you're interested in what I do, I'm glad you're here!

I was born in Ontario, Canada, but I've lived in San Jose, California for most of my life. I always tell my friends that I wish there was lots of snow around me, but then I think about having mounds of it everywhere and realize that after a month or so of this, it'll probably deteriorate into this...

I am pursuing a B.S. in Computer Science at the University of Southern Californa in Los Angeles, and I graduate in 2019. Ironically, before my summer of senior year in high school, I wasn't interested in computer science at all — I really wanted to become a doctor. So, I co-founded a non-profit organization that makes 3D printed prosthetics. Now, although I am fully engaged in programming, my passion for the healthcare industry still exists, and fuels my interest in using the skills I have to help people improve their lives.

No matter who you are, I want to help you create a better world for the future.

Feel free to poke around and check out what I do. Take a look at my work here!

Work

Over the years, I have been involved in a lot of organizations and activities on campus, including but not limited to (and in no particular order):

  • Co-founding the first Technology Fellowship Program in Los Angeles that brings together 8 undergraduate students, 6 startups, and 3 venture capital firms in Los Angeles.
  • Developing a product in Lavalab, USC's student run product incubator, and pitching to an audience of 300 and 4 judges.
  • Performing signal processing and audio learning research in the Media Informatics and Content Analysis (MICA) Lab at USC.
  • Working with a team of 6 on a funded startup.
  • Organizing a hackathon.

My interests lie in entrepreneurship, software development, and deep learning, and I hope to build on those concepts as much as I can to make the world a better place.
